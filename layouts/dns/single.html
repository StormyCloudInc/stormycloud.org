What is DNS?

DNS (the Domain Name System) is the internet's address book. Every time you visit a website, your device queries a DNS resolver to translate the domain name into an IP address your browser can connect to. Most people use their ISP's default DNS, which often logs every site you visit and may censor or filter results.

StormyCloud's public DNS resolvers are different. We maintain a strict zero-logging policy and don't record your queries, your IP address, or any identifying information. Our servers support DNSSEC validation to protect against spoofing and tampering, and we never filter or censor results. It's DNS the way it should be: fast, private, and unfiltered.

DNS Endpoints

DNS (TCP / UDP)
Operational

Standard DNS resolvers supporting both IPv4 and IPv6. Configure these in your OS network settings or router for instant privacy.

IPv4 23.128.248.2 23.128.248.4
IPv6 2602:FC05::2 2602:FC05::4
DNS-over-HTTPS (DoH)
Operational

Encrypts DNS queries over HTTPS, preventing eavesdropping and man-in-the-middle attacks. Supported natively in most modern browsers and operating systems.

URL https://dns.stormycloud.org/dns-query
DNS-over-TLS (DoT)
Operational

Encrypts DNS queries over a dedicated TLS connection on port 853. Widely supported by Android's Private DNS feature, systemd-resolved, and other DNS clients.

Server dns.stormycloud.org:853
